Oakland Mayoral Campaign Breakdowns:

Cash Raised, Cash Spent, and Warchests, 1/1/2013-12/31/2013

How might you run a mayoral campaign differently if more of your funds came from loans and debt instead of straight contributions? Have you considered that different funding sources may affect a campaign's focus and interests? Such information is available in public record filings and may offer a more data-driven perspective on a campaign's priorities and promises than the usual stump speech or talking points, but it is often too opaque or too technical to be consumed by the average citizen.

In this hackathon project, we visualize the financial summaries of the 2014 mayoral campaigns for the city of Oakland, CA. We hope these "financial fingerprints" make technical campaign finance details more consumable. With greater accessibility, we hope to promote questions about campaigns' strategies. Who has received more non-monetary gifts? To what extend might these impact a campaign's operations? Might they reflect a candidate's political connections, or are they even relevant? What other questions can public data help answer? Tweet #OpenData

Financial Fingerprints

A campaign's cash balance is the sum of all monetary contributions and loans, minus expenditures*. Campaigns can additionally accrue unpaid expenses, although these liabilities are not counted in the cash balance. Finally, campaigns are required to post the value of non-monetary contributions, such as gifts and donations. Together these factors sum to the effective balance of a campaign.

*: Total expenditures also include loans made, although this line item has been excluded as no campaigns have filed any loans made.

Data and Credits

The above charts visualize CA Form 460 summaries for all 2014 Oakland mayoral campaigns which have filed Form 460's for the period 1/1/2013 - 12/31/2013. The next batch of Form 460 updates will be available June 2014. Public records are available at the City of Oakland's Public Portal for Campaign Finance Disclosure.
